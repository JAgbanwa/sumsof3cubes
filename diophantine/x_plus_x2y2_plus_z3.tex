\title{Integer Solutions to $x + x^2 y^2 + z^3 = 0$}
\author{}
\date{}
\maketitle

\section*{Result}
All integer solutions are:
\[
(0,y,0),\quad (-z^3,0,z),
\]
and the sporadic solutions
\[
(-1,\pm1,0),\; (-1,\pm3,-2),\; (-8,\pm39,-46).
\]

\section*{Proof}
Rewrite the equation as
\[
z^3 = -x(1 + xy^2).
\]
Since $\gcd(x,1+xy^2)=1$, both factors must be cubes up to sign. This yields the infinite families and reduces the remaining cases to a Mordell-type equation, whose only integer solutions are the listed sporadic points.
